How to Get a Temporary Resident Visa

Step-by-Step: Temporary Resident Visa Application

1
Schedule Consulate Appointment

Contact your nearest Mexican consulate to schedule an appointment. Processing times vary by location; book 2-4 weeks in advance.

Find consulate: SRE Consulate Directory

2
Gather Required Documents

Prepare the following:

  • Valid passport (6+ months validity)
  • Completed visa application form
  • Passport-size photo (white background)
  • Proof of financial solvency (see requirements below)
  • Visa fee payment ($50-$80 USD varies by consulate)
3
Prove Financial Solvency

You must demonstrate economic independence through ONE of these methods:[5]

Option A - Bank Statements: Show average monthly balance of ~$69,750 USD (5,000x UMA at 113.14 MXN) for the last 12 months

Option B - Monthly Income: Prove monthly income of ~$4,185 USD (~83,640 MXN at 300x MDW of 278.80 MXN) for last 6 months

Option C - Property: Proof of real estate ownership in Mexico valued at minimum ~$558,000 USD (40,000x UMA)

Note: Amounts are updated annually. Verify current requirements at your consulate.

4
Attend Consulate Interview

Bring all documents to your appointment. The consul will review your application and may ask about your plans in Mexico. If approved, you'll receive a visa sticker in your passport valid for 180 days to enter Mexico.

5
Enter Mexico & Exchange Visa

Within 30 days of arrival, visit your local INM office to exchange your visa for a Resident Card. In San Luis Potos√≠, the INM office is located at:

INM San Luis Potos√≠
Av. Venustiano Carranza 2395, Zona Centro
Tel: (444) 812-3556

‚úì
Receive Your Resident Card

After biometrics and document verification (usually 2-4 weeks), you'll receive your Temporary Resident Card valid for 1 year, renewable up to 4 years total.

Important Warning

Do NOT attempt to work remotely on a tourist visa while earning income from clients in Mexico. While enforcement is limited, this technically violates immigration law. If you plan to work (remotely or otherwise), obtain proper residency status. Many expats use the Temporary Resident visa which allows you to apply for a work permit.[5]

Path to Permanent Residency

After 4 consecutive years as a Temporary Resident, you can apply for Permanent Residency. Alternatively, you can qualify directly for Permanent Residency through:[5]

  • Retirement: Proving pension/retirement income of ~$7,100 USD/month (or ~$280,000 USD in savings)
  • Family ties: Marriage to a Mexican citizen or having Mexican children
  • Humanitarian reasons: Special circumstances evaluated by INM
  • Points system: Based on professional qualifications, investments, or family

10 Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to speak Spanish to live in San Luis Potos√≠?

While you can get by with English in tourist areas and some businesses, Spanish is essential for daily life in SLP. Unlike San Miguel de Allende or Canc√∫n, there's no large English-speaking infrastructure here. Basic Spanish will significantly improve your experience. Many expats take Spanish classes at local language schools (CELE at UASLP, or private tutors for ~$10-15 USD/hour).

Can I work remotely on a tourist visa?

Technically, working while on a tourist visa (even remotely for foreign clients) is a gray area in Mexican law. While enforcement is minimal for remote workers earning from abroad, the safest approach is to obtain Temporary Residency if you plan to stay long-term and work. This also provides access to banking, healthcare, and other services.

How does healthcare quality compare to the US/Europe?

Private healthcare in Mexico is generally excellent. Many doctors trained in the US or Europe, and facilities like Hospital √Ångeles meet international standards. For routine care, you'll find quality comparable to developed countries at a fraction of the cost. For complex procedures, some expats travel to Mexico City or Guadalajara for specialists, though SLP has capable doctors in most fields.

Is the tap water safe to drink?

No. Like most of Mexico, you should not drink tap water in SLP. Use garrafones (20L jugs of purified water) delivered to your home (~35-50 MXN each) or install a filtration system. Most locals and expats use garrafones‚Äîdelivery services will swap empties for full ones regularly. Ice in restaurants is generally safe (made with purified water).

What's the expat community like?

SLP has a small but growing expat community, primarily consisting of: (1) professionals working for international companies (BMW, GM suppliers, aerospace), (2) retirees seeking affordable living, (3) digital nomads and remote workers, (4) spouses of Mexican nationals. Unlike San Miguel de Allende, you won't find large organized expat groups, but there are Facebook groups and informal meetups. Many expats appreciate the more authentic integration with Mexican culture.

How's the weather throughout the year?

SLP enjoys a semi-arid climate with mild temperatures year-round. Summers (May-September) are warm with afternoon rain showers‚Äîhighs around 28-32¬∞C (82-90¬∞F). Winters (November-February) are mild and dry‚Äîhighs around 20-24¬∞C (68-75¬∞F), cool nights around 5-10¬∞C (41-50¬∞F). The altitude (1,864m) keeps temperatures moderate even in summer. Bring layers for winter evenings.

Can I bring my pets to Mexico?

Yes! Mexico is pet-friendly. Requirements: (1) Health certificate from your vet (issued within 10 days of travel), (2) Current rabies vaccination (15 days to 1 year before travel), (3) For dogs/cats, no import permit needed. Airlines have their own pet policies. Many apartments allow pets (verify before signing). SLP has good veterinary care‚Äîexpect $30-50 USD for routine visits.

How do I pay Mexican taxes as an expat?

Tax residency is complex‚Äîconsult a Mexican accountant (contador). Generally: if you spend 183+ days/year in Mexico, you may become a tax resident. Mexico taxes worldwide income for residents. US citizens face additional complexity with FATCA reporting. Many expats work with accountants experienced in expat taxation‚Äîexpect $200-500 USD/year for basic tax services. Income tax rates: 1.92% to 35% depending on income level.[10]

Are there international schools?

Yes, SLP has several bilingual and international school options: (1) Instituto Potosino - bilingual curriculum, (2) Colegio Motolinia - Catholic, bilingual, (3) American School of San Luis Potos√≠, (4) Various Montessori schools. Tuition ranges from $3,000-$10,000 USD/year depending on the school. Most teach in Spanish with English language instruction.

What's the nightlife and social scene like?

SLP has a vibrant social scene centered around the historic center. You'll find craft beer bars, mezcaler√≠as, live music venues, and clubs. Popular areas: Callej√≥n de San Francisco, Plaza del Carmen, and various rooftop bars. The city is also a university town (UASLP), so there's a young, energetic atmosphere. Cultural events, concerts, and festivals happen year-round.

Can I use my US/foreign driver's license?

Foreign driver's licenses are valid for tourists (up to 6 months). Once you become a resident, you should obtain a Mexican license. In San Luis Potos√≠, this requires: (1) Proof of residency, (2) Medical exam, (3) Written test, (4) Practical driving test. The process is straightforward and costs around $50-100 USD total.

How do I ship my belongings to Mexico?

Options: (1) Menaje de casa (household goods import) - residents can import used items duty-free once within 6 months of obtaining residency. (2) International movers (estimate $3,000-8,000 USD for a full container from US). (3) Many expats sell everything and buy new in Mexico‚Äîfurniture is affordable here.
