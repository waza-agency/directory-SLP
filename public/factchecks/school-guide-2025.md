- **Corroborating Source 1:** [WENR Education Reports](https://wenr.wes.org/) - Mexican education system overview

**CONFIDENCE:** High - Constitutional amendment documented

### CLAIM 7: Foreign Document Requirements

**CLAIM:** "Foreign documents need apostille + certified translation"

**VERDICT:** ✅ TRUE

**INVESTIGATION SUMMARY:**
Mexico joined the Hague Apostille Convention in 1995. Standard requirement for foreign educational credentials includes apostille and certified Spanish translation.

**EVIDENCE CHAIN:**
- **Primary Source:** [Mexperience.com](https://www.mexperience.com/) - Foreign document legalization guide
- **Corroborating Source:** Official Apostille Guidelines for Mexico

**CONFIDENCE:** High - Standard legal requirement

## PATTERN ANALYSIS

### Accuracy Patterns
The article demonstrated mixed accuracy:
- **Educational system information:** Highly accurate (preschool mandate, preparatoria mandate, document requirements)
- **Individual school details:** Multiple errors including non-existent schools
- **Statistics:** Significant inflation in UASLP numbers
- **Addresses:** Several required correction

### Critical Issues Identified
1. **Non-existent schools listed:** Two schools (Instituto Thomas Jefferson, Instituto Lux) were listed but do not operate in SLP
2. **Inflated statistics:** UASLP enrollment and ranking significantly overstated
3. **Address inaccuracies:** Multiple school addresses were incorrect
4. **Unverified claims:** Some certifications (Cambridge) could not be verified

### Bias Indicators
**Positive bias toward school quantity/quality:**
- Original listing inflated number of international schools
- University statistics appeared to use outdated or incorrect data
- Some schools listed without verification of SLP presence

### Sourcing Quality
The original article lacked citation of specific sources. Our investigation relied on:
- Official school websites
- International Baccalaureate database (IB.org)
- QS World University Rankings
- Mexican government education portals (SEP, gob.mx)
